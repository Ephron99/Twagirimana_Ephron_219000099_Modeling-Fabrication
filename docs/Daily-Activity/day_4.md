# Materials & Fabrication Methods

#### Overview 

This session focused on understanding the relationship between materials and fabrication methods and how this relationship affects the final design outcome. In digital fabrication, materials are not simply passive elements; they directly influence how a design is created, manufactured, and used.

Different materials have unique properties such as strength, flexibility, thickness, durability, and thermal resistance. These properties determine which fabrication techniques can be applied and how a product performs over time.

#### Objective


- Understand how material properties influence design
- Explore how different materials behave during fabrication
- Learn how to choose materials based on fabrication methods
- Gain hands-on experience through material-based prototyping

In digital fabrication, selecting the correct material is essential because it affects Strength, Flexibility, Durability, and Machinability.

#### Common Materials Used in Digital Fabrication
- Wood


- Acrylic (Perspex)


- Plastics


- Metal (Aluminum / Steel)

During the session, swe  explored how different materials behave when subjected to fabrication processes. This hands-on approach helped demonstrate how design choices must adapt to the properties of the selected material.

From this session I gained that Materials play an active role in design and fabrication. materials are not simply passive components but essential factors that influence the entire fabrication process. By understanding how materials interact with fabrication techniques, designers can create more efficient, durable, and functional products.
